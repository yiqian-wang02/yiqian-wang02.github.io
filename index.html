YYiqian Wang 王逸骞

Linguistics PhD student · Harvard University

Research interests: I am a second-year PhD student in the Linguistics Department at Harvard, specializing in semantics, pragmatics, and their interfaces. My research interests center on the typology of inferences – how they are represented and how they arise. I am currently working on projects investigating presuppositions, focusing on how they project across disjunction and how they are triggered by certain types of words. I approach these questions through a combination of formal linguistic theory and human behavioral experiments, and am trying to incorporate methods from information theory and computational modeling. More broadly, I am fascinated by the expressive power of natural language and the relative reliability of communication.

Before coming to Harvard, I completed my undergraduate study at Tsinghua University (2020 September – 2024 June), where I built a background in linguistics, philosophical logic, and English literature.
